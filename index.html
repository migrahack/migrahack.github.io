APRIL 16, 2014

On this day, Philadelphia's then mayor Michael Nutter signed the Executive Order 1-14, a policy to prohibit the city from holding an individual for US Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) or notifying ICE of their presence in police custody without a judicial warrant.

THE START OF THE FIGHT

On December 11, 2013, a local immigrant rights organization Juntos gathered in front of the local ICE office in Philadelphia, blocking vehicles exits to prevent the transfer of detained immigrants, essentially shutting down ICE for the day.

They proclaimed Ni Una Más, or Not 1 More and called for an end to ICE holds, deportations and the Secure Communities Program, which is a collaboration between ICE and police.
